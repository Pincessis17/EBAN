The Problem

carry real risk

Today's reality
—

Someone posts in a WhatsApp group asking if anyone travelling from home can carry a package.

—

No written agreement, no fee structure, no accountability — and no protection if the item is lost.

—

—

The sender has no recourse if the item is damaged or never arrives.

EBAN doesn't eliminate this behaviour — it structures it.

People from mainland Africa living in Mauritius regularly need personal items, food, and documents brought from home. This happens today through informal channels that leave everyone exposed.

We formalise the trust, add accountability at every step, and reward the traveller for their service.

“A structured process with clear rules, a transparent fee structure, physical handoff at the airport, and an incentive for the traveller.”
The Process

Five steps from
request to collection

Every delivery follows a clearly defined five-stage process. Both sender and traveller know exactly what is expected — and when.

01

Request Submission

Sender describes item, weight, and value. Service fee is paid upfront before matching begins.

02

Traveller Matching

Eban identifies a verified traveller on your corridor and presents the delivery request. Both parties are notified via WhatsApp.

03

Pre-departure Handoff

Sender hands item to traveller at origin. Traveller inspects and confirms. Both parties receive confirmation from EBAN.

04

Airport Collection

On arrival in Mauritius, EBAN meets the traveller outside arrivals. Receipt is signed. The traveller's obligation ends here.

05

Collection

Sender is notified and collects their items. Students collect at their university. Everyone else collects at the Eban Main Office, African Leadership College of Higher Education.

What We Carry

Clear rules,
no surprises

Eligible Items

  • ✓Non-perishable, sealed food products
  • ✓Personal care & hygiene products
  • ✓Clothing and fabric
  • ✓Documents and printed materials
  • ✓Small household goods (within weight limit)

Prohibited Items

  • ✕Cash, cheques or financial instruments
  • ✕Controlled substances or unprescribed pharmaceuticals
  • ✕Weapons or items prohibited under Mauritius customs law
  • ✕Rice in any form (restricted under Mauritius import regulations)
  • ✕Perishable food requiring refrigeration
  • ✕Any item the traveller is not comfortable carrying

Dedicated Extra Bag — Fully Covered by Eban

When items exceed a traveller's free luggage allowance, Eban books and pays for a dedicated extra bag on their ticket. The bag cost is shared across senders based on weight. The traveller pays nothing — all costs are covered by sender fees. For larger shipments we coordinate across multiple travellers — contact us to discuss.

Trust & Safety

Accountability
at every step

Verified Identities

Every traveller submits a valid ID and flight booking confirmation before being matched to any sender. All senders are registered and verified by the Eban team before their request is activated.

Full Transaction Log

EBAN maintains a complete record of every delivery — item description, declared weight, traveller name, and timestamps from request to final collection.

Traveller Protection

Travellers may refuse any item without penalty. EBAN ensures full compliance with Mauritius Customs and Revenue Authority requirements and maintains a clear dispute escalation path.
